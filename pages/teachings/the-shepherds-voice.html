Hearing the Shepherd's Voice

The Bible introduces us to the metaphor of the shepherd and his sheep. The Shepherd in the Scriptures is no ordinary caretaker. He bonds with the sheep as a mother bonds with her new-born child, showing affection, nurturing their needs, and gently guiding them to safety. As a baby finds comfort by hearing her mother's voice, so does the child of God respond to the voice of their Heavenly Father. Our Shepherd does not see a flock but individual sheep who have unique names. He tells them apart and knows their distinct needs.

He will tend his flock like a shepherd; he will gather the lambs in his arms; he will carry them in his bosom, and gently lead those that are with young. (Isaiah 40:11)

My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me (John 10:27).

The sheep hear his voice, and he calls his own sheep by name and leads them out. When he has brought out all his own, he goes before them, and the sheep follow him, for they know his voice. I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep (John 10:3-4,11)
