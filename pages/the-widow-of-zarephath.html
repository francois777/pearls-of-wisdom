Elijah and the Widow of Zarephath

Read the story of how God miraculously provided for the prophet Elijah and the widow who shared her last meal with him. (1 Kings 16:29-17:24)

Ahab was a very evil king

In Israel, there lived a very evil king called Ahab. He was even worse than all the kings that were before him. Ahab did not choose a woman from Israel. He was more interested in Jezebel, a lady from a foreign country. Ahab's choice caused the nation Israel much trouble. King Ahab built an altar to the idol, Baal, and he also erected a statute for a goddess. His careless actions went against everything that Israel was supposed to believe. Therefore, it is no surprise that the whole nation was about to suffer because of their irresponsible king.

God raised up a righteous man, Elijah, to pronounce judgement over Ahab. The Holy Spirit gave Elijah the courage to bring this message to Ahab: "From now on, there will be neither rain, nor dew in your country. You, oh king, cannot do anything about it. It will only rain when I command rain to fall." This message made Ahab very angry, but God extraordinarily protected Elijah. He told Elijah to go and hide at a brook, east of the Jordan River.

God told Ahab to hide at a brook

God commanded the ravens to feed Elijah

Elijah had plenty of clean water to wash and drink, and God also planned for meals. He commanded the ravens to take food to Elijah - bread and meat in the morning and in the evening. Elijah trusted God and knew that Israel would repent and turn back to God after the drought. After a while, Elijah's faith was challenged because the persistent lack of rain caused the brook to dry up.

God did not take his hand off the prophet, and Elijah was about to see an even greater miracle. God told Elijah to walk to Zarephath to find a widow. Elijah did not know how poor the widow was, and all he knew was that God had already told the widow to feed Elijah.

Elijah arrived at the gate of Zarephath and saw a woman gathering sticks. He knew this was the widow God told him about. Elijah called the widow and said to her, "Bring me a little water in a vessel that I may drink." Then, Elijah added, "Bring me a morsel of bread in your hand." The woman did not yet understand Elijah and had little faith. Elijah had no idea that he was asking her for her last food. The widow told Elijah about her predicament: "As the LORD your God lives, I have nothing baked, only a handful of flour in a jar and a little oil in a jug. And now I am gathering a couple of sticks that I may go in and prepare it for myself and my son, that we may eat it and die." She actually tried to explain to Elijah that she and her son would die of hunger after eating their last meal.

Elijah found the widow gathering sticks

Elijah had no intention to rob that lady, and God revealed his plan to Elijah. The prophet spoke words of comfort and hope to the widow: "Do not fear; go and do as you have said. But first make me a little cake of it and bring it to me, and afterward make something for yourself and your son. For thus says the LORD, the God of Israel, ‘The jar of flour shall not be spent, and the jug of oil shall not be empty, until the day that the LORD sends rain upon the earth.’" Amazingly, God promised to provide for the widow until the drought had ended.

It happened just as Elijah had told the widow. She could feed Elijah, and there was abundant food in her house until the drought ended. Amid crisis and distress, God provided abundantly in an impossible situation. Moreover, God's revelation of goodness to the home of the widow did not end here. He allowed a very distressing sickness to come over the widow's son. The boy became so sick that he could hardly breath.

The widow's son became very ill

The widow turned against the prophet

Because the woman's faith was so small, she turned against the prophet and said: "You have come to me to bring my sin to remembrance and to cause the death of my son!". Elijah was also very disturbed and knew they needed to trust God again for a miracle. He asked the woman to hand her son to him, and he took the child upstairs to his room. Elijah cried to God, and he showed his faith by stretching himself on the child, praying earnestly, "O LORD my God, let this child's life come into him again."

God heard the outcry of Elijah and healed the child completely. Elijah took the child downstairs with gratefulness, handing his mother a healthy child. When the widow saw what happened, she really believed and said to Elijah: "Now I know that you are a man of God, and that the word of the LORD in your mouth is truth." The house was filled with gladness, and the three of them shared a meal with grateful hearts.
