CREATED IN THE IMAGE OF GOD

The story of your life started even before you were born. When your mother conceived you, you were already in God's mind. Take note of these words of David, the Psalmist: "For you formed my inward parts; you knitted me together in my mother's womb" (Psalm 139:13). You were planned by God and designed by God. He created you in His image (Genesis 1:27) to live in fellowship with Him (Philippians 3:8).

Being created by God means that you are significant. You have a purpose. You have a destiny, and you belong to your Heavenly Father. He will not reject you. "And the LORD, He is the One who goes before you. He will be with you; He will not leave you nor forsake you; do not fear nor be dismayed” (Deuteronomy 31:8). If you received Jesus Christ, you are a child of God.

But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God, who were born, not of blood nor of the will of the flesh nor of the will of man, but of God (John 1:12).

When I become a child of God, my Heavenly Father is my new reference point. Everything He says about me tells me who I am. Even if everyone else rejects me, it does not matter any more. I have an inheritance that is mine forever.

But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born of woman, born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law, so that we might receive adoption as sons. And because you are sons, God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, crying, “Abba! Father!” So you are no longer a slave, but a son, and if a son, then an heir through God. (Galations 4:4-7)

I invite you to respond to this realisation by thanking God for what He has done for you. On the cross Jesus removed the barrier that prevented spontaneous communication and direct access to God. See the invitation directly from God's Word:

Let us then with confidence draw near to the throne of grace, that we may receive mercy and find grace to help in time of need. (Hebrews 4:16)
