WE ARE AFFECTED BY WHAT HAPPENED IN PARADICE

Paradice was both a catastrophe and a demonstration of Yahweh's goodness. It is possible that the creation narrative was contemplated by God for a long time. God knew that giving humankind a free choice involved the test of His eternal love and goodness.

And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, "You may surely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die." (Genesis 2:16-17)

Human reasoning could suggest that God had to choose between righteousness and love. However, God's goodness upheld both His righteousness and his love. His righteousness demanded that Adam's sin resulted in death. But the love of God moved him in an amazing demonstration of grace.

Adam was hopeless. His disobedient was disastrous because God had spelt out the consequences clearly. Nevertheless, God demonstrated His love for Adam, and the whole of humankind, in an unimaginable way. Christ decided to die in our place. The unthinkable act of love was first prophesied by the prophet Isiah.

But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned – every one – to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all. (Isaiah 53:5,6)

Yet it was the will of the LORD to crush him; he has put him to grief; when his soul makes an offering for guilt, he shall see his offspring; he shall prolong his days; the will of the LORD shall prosper in his hand. (Isaiah 53:10)

Then, after the death and resurrection of Christ, the apostle Paul reflected on what Christ had accomplished to restore humankind to himself..

...so that Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith — that you, being rooted and grounded in love, may have strength to comprehend with all the saints what is the breadth and length and height and depth, and to know the love of Christ that surpasses knowledge, that you may be filled with all the fullness of God. (Ephesians 3:17-19)
