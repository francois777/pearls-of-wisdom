WE ARE AFFECTED BY WHAT HAPPENED IN PARADICE

Paradice was both a catastrophe and a demonstration of Yahweh's goodness. It is possible that the creation narrative was contemplated by God for a long time. God knew that giving humankind a free choice involved the test of His eternal love and goodness.

And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, "You may surely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die." (Genesis 2:16-17)

Human reasoning could suggest that God had to choose between righteousness and love. However, God's goodness upheld His righteous and his love. His righteousness demanded that Adam's sin result in death. But the love of God moved him in an amasing demonstration of grace. Adam was hopeless, but God demonstrated His love for him (and humankind) in an unimaginable way. Christ decided to die in our place.
