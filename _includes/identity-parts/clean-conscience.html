HOW TO FOLLOW JESUS WITH A CLEAN CONSCIENCE

Living with a clean conscience is a bridge that every Christian must cross at some stage in their life. God gave us a conscience to make us aware of the voice of the Holy Spirit. If we are not able to hear God's voice, we can't follow His directions. We must therefore learn about our spirit nature.

When God takes possession of my heart, the Holy Spirit also convicts me of sin. He fills me with remorse for all rebellion in my life. He also gives me a genuine desire to repent of everything that offends the Holy Spirit. He wants to dwell in me, but He wants to live in a clean temple. Who can imagine that Almighty God wants to live in the heart of a human being? Recall the conversation between Jesus and Nicodemus:

"Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God." Nicodemus said to him, "How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter a second time into his mother's womb and be born?" Jesus answered, "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit." (John 3:3-6)

When Christ enters my heart, the Holy Spirit confronts me of any iniquity in my life. Some of us struggle to live in obedience and often do not find the courage to do what we should be doing.

If you identify with this battle, I want to encourage you that God has something greater in store for you! Consider the striking metaphor of the victorious life in Hebrews 4. It speaks of entering the Sabbath rest just as Joshua gave his people rest after entering the promised land.

So then, there remains a Sabbath rest for the people of God, for whoever has entered God's rest has also rested from his works as God did from his. (Hebrews 4:9-10)

Do you know that your conscience is your friend? It makes you feel uneasy when you suppress the voice of the Holy Spirit. This is how the Holy Spirit uses your conscience to get your attention. To end this battle, you must repent! You must come to the place where you hate every evidence of sin in your life. You need to hate the distractions in your life that feed your sin nature. Christ wants to be on the throne of your life. You must die to sin to live! Paul understood this battle, and he tells us how he found victory.

I have been crucified with Christ. It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me. And the life I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave himself for me. (Galatians 2:20)

Paul had to die so that Christ could live in Him! I urge you, dear brother and sister in Christ, to discover the secret of life in Romans 8:1-17. Would you please read it like a prospector carefully looks for a diamond? Paul teaches here about living without condemnation, walking according to the Spirit, experiencing life and peace, pleasing God, being led by the Spirit, and becoming heirs of God and fellow heirs with Christ.

Jesus is calling us to a place of total surrender. He wants to dwell in us as our Comforter. He wants to walk with me and talk with me. There is no other way to victory. We were born to have continual fellowship with the living God. We were meant to be sons and daughters of God!

The conviction of the Holy Spirit calls us to repentance and life, but the condemnation of the adversary leads to bondage and death.

"Humble yourselves, therefore, under the mighty hand of God so that at the proper time he may exalt you, casting all your anxieties on him, because he cares for you." (I Peter 5:6)
