Introduction

When you accept Jesus Christ and believe in Him, God grants you the right to be His child (John 1:12). You are born again and reconciled with God. Everything in your life becomes new (2 Corinthians 5:17-20). The Holy Spirit dwells in you, renewing your spirit, and enabling you to hear the voice of your Shepherd.

Unity with the Father

Jesus uses the metaphor of the vine and its branches. The branches naturally bear fruit because they draw nutrition from the vine. The vineyard speaks of dependency and unity. Your spirit is the branch that is planted in the vine. Jesus is the Vine, and His Spirit is the Comforter who dwells in you. He talks to you and imparts His desires in you.

Jesus Christ, your Saviour and Friend, gives you safety, protection, provision, guidance, understanding and trust. This relationship is for you! Do you need more reason to submit to the guidance of the Great Shepherd?

He is so near you

Dear reader, are you longing to live closer to God? Paul eagerly wanted to "know him and the power of his resurrection" (Philippians 3:10). But he was not God's only child. You and I must also pursue this companionship. Won't you start listening to God's voice from deep inside you, and become aware of the One who abides in you?

For what great nation is there that has a god so near to it as the LORD our God is to us, whenever we call upon him? (Deuteronomy 4:7)

God is inviting you to get to know Him more intimately. Reach out to your Heavenly Father through faith and open your heart to hear from Him. Talk to God right where you are. Tell God that you love Him and appreciate Him.

On the last day of the feast, the great day, Jesus stood up and cried out, “If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink” (John 7:37).

Come, everyone who thirsts, come to the waters (Isaiah 55:1).

I endeavour to show you how God has spoken throughout the ages. As you continue to read, you will understand why He wants you to hear His voice, and you will learn to listen expectantly and overcome any hindrances from hearing His voice.

Do you remember the story of Samuel? Eli, the high priest, told Samuel: "if he calls you, you shall say, 'Speak, LORD, for your servant hears’". Won't you, just like Samuel, answer God today, saying ‘Speak, LORD, your servant hears’ (I Samuel 3:9-10).

Reflection

  1. What does the voice of a shepherd mean to the sheep?
  2. What does it mean to you that Jesus calls His sheep by their names?
  3. In the parable of the vine and the branches (John 15), what do the branches tell us about our part in producing quality fruit?
  4. Could you undertake, in agreement with your friends, to spend a few minutes a day, praying and worshipping God, for a period of one or two weeks? Then, discuss with your friends how it impacted your life.
