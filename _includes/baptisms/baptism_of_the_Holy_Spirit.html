3. Baptism of the Holy Spirit

Recall what John the Baptist told the people about the One who would come after him: He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and fire. John the Baptist baptised with water but Jesus baptised with the Holy Spirit and fire. Keep in mind that the disciples of Jesus had already been born again. We read in John 20:22 how Jesus breathed on them, saying "Receive the Holy Spirit". Pentecost happened afterwards. Luke wrote about Pentecost as the promise of the Father:

Image by michel kwan from Pixabay

And while staying with them he ordered them not to depart from Jerusalem, but to wait for the promise of the Father, which, he said, “you heard from me; for John baptized with water, but you will be baptized with the Holy Spirit not many days from now” (Acts 1:4,5).

In the description of what happened on the day of Pentecost, we read again about the fire that was prophesied. This kind of baptism was initiated by the Holy Spirit and it's purpose was to enable the disciples to boldly proclaim the good news about Jesus Christ. Up to this stage, the Jews were not concerned about the salvation of the nations but this was about to change. God elected Israel for a purpose, much greater than the preservation of their own identity. Christ, the Messiah, came to save the whole world from their sins.

When the day of Pentecost arrived, they were all together in one place. And suddenly there came from heaven a sound like a mighty rushing wind, and it filled the entire house where they were sitting. And divided tongues as of fire appeared to them and rested on each one of them. And they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues as the Spirit gave them utterance. (Acts 2:1-4)

After the day of Pentecost, the disciples continued to teach the Jews that the same Jesus they had crucified came to fulfil the prophecies in the Scripture about the promised Messiah. In Acts 4 we continue to read how Peter and John boldly proclaimed the message of the resurrection of the dead. They were then filled by the Holy Spirit.

And when they had prayed, the place in which they were gathered together was shaken, and they were all filled with the Holy Spirit and continued to speak the word of God with boldness (Acts 4:31).

When Paul arrived at Ephesus, he came across disciples who were baptised by John the Baptist. These disciples were not yet taught about the Holy Spirit. Paul then explained to them that the baptism of John actually pointed to Jesus who baptised with the Holy Spirit. They gladly received Paul's message!

On hearing this, they were baptized in the name of the Lord Jesus. And when Paul had laid his hands on them, the Holy Spirit came on them, and they began speaking in tongues and prophesying (Acts 19:5-6).
