THE RESURRECTION OF THE DEAD

The resurrection of Jesus from the dead and the subsequent pouring out of the Holy Spirit was the birth of the church. However, these events also distinguished Christianity from all known religions on earth. It was not just another attempt of human kind to reach out to God in order to gain some kind of acceptance. No human being was involved in the resurrection, rather, it was the loving and gracious initiative of God to reach out to all peoples. All people were completely seperated from God because of sin and everyone desperately needed God's intervention to give hope to a dying world. The resurrection of Jesus was also the foundation of a new relationship between God and man. Christianity is not a religion, but a relationship that would last until eternity!

Every part of the human life of Jesus was prophesied thousands of years before it happened, and the resurrection of Jesus was no acception. The way in which it happened was also extra-ordinary. The book of Matthew relates how an angel rolled away the stone at the entrance of the grave:

And behold, there was a great earthquake, for an angel of the Lord descended from heaven and came and rolled back the stone and sat on it. His appearance was like lightning, and his clothing white as snow. And for fear of him the guards trembled and became like dead men. In 1 Corinthians 15, the apostle Paul reflects on the mission of Jesus Christ, that he died for our sins but rose again on the third day. He then appeard to his disciples and also to many others, including "more than five hundred brothers at one time". The resurrection of Christ is also our hope and first evidence of eternal life with Him.

But in fact Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep. For as by a man came death, by a man has come also the resurrection of the dead. For as in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive. But each in his own order: Christ the firstfruits, then at his coming those who belong to Christ (1 Corinthians 15:20-23).

The resurrection of the believers is followed by the glorious announcement of a new kingdom: "Then comes the end, when he delivers the kingdom to God the Father after destroying every rule and every authority and power. For he must reign until he has put all his enemies under his feet. The last enemy to be destroyed is death"() (1 Cor 15:24-26).

In his letter to the Philippians, the apostle Paul says that he found "the righteousness from God that depends on faith". The resurrection power of God also has a significance for our spiritual life and indicates the victory that Christ secured for us.
