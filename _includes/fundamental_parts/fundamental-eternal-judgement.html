ETERNAL JUDGMENT

The book of Revelation describes the final judgment, also called the 'white throne judgment'. It is the condemnation of the ungodly to a lake of fire.

Then I saw a great white throne and him who was seated on it. From his presence earth and sky fled away, and no place was found for them. And I saw the dead, great and small, standing before the throne, and books were opened. Then another book was opened, which is the book of life. And the dead were judged by what was written in the books, according to what they had done. And the sea gave up the dead who were in it, Death and Hades gave up the dead who were in them, and they were judged, each one of them, according to what they had done. Then Death and Hades were thrown into the lake of fire. This is the second death, the lake of fire. And if anyone's name was not found written in the book of life, he was thrown into the lake of fire. (Revelation 20:11-15)

People who only have a superficial knowledge of the Bible would question the severity of such a judgment. However, the crucifixion of God's only Son was also severe. Jesus Christ took on Himself the sin of the world (John 1:29) so that He could endure the wages of sin on our behalf. This was the price that Jesus paid to give us the gift of eternal life (Romans 6:22-23).

The doctrine of Eternal Judgment can only be understood by grasping the holiness of Jahweh. His uprightness and purity is so brilliant in perfection, it is far removed and completely seperated from sin. Read the prophet Isaiah's vision of the Lord and the affect the vision had on him (Isaiah 6:1-6).

We read in Acts 17:30-31 that God "commands all people everywhere to repent, because he has fixed a day on which he will judge the world in righteousness". God ordained from the beginning that sin be judged. It is imperitive that we understand who God is. His holiness draws us into devoted relationship with Him. There is no compromise and no excuse. The history of Israel tells us how God hates sin and how He desires complete devotion to him.

The Bible teaches about the "Day of judgement", a specific day when everybody will be held accountable.

I tell you, on the day of judgment people will give account for every careless word they speak, for by your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned” (Matthew 12:36).

“When the Son of Man comes in his glory, and all the angels with him, then he will sit on his glorious throne. Before him will be gathered all the nations, and he will separate people one from another as a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats" (Matthew 25:31-32).

Then he will answer them, saying, 'Truly, I say to you, as you did not do it to one of the least of these, you did not do it to me.' And these will go away into eternal punishment, but the righteous into eternal life (Matthew 25:45-46).

The miraculous rescue of Noah and his family from the flood was symbolic of the hope that the righteous have in an unrighteous world. The rest of the ancient world at that time were destroyed by the flood. Similarly, Lot was rescued while Sodom and Gomorrah was "condemned to extinction, making them an example of what is going to happen to the ungodly" (2 Peter 2:6).

Here is the lessons for us today: "if he rescued righteous Lot" (2 Peter 2:7), "then the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trials, and to keep the unrighteous under punishment until the day of judgment" (2 Peter 2:9).
